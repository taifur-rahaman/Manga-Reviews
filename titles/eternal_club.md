## Overview

"Eternal Club" is a supernatural sci-fi manhua about Lu Li, an ordinary worker who gains the extraordinary ability to buy and sell "Life" and "Lifespan" from people, and to manipulate time. With this newfound power, he sets out to create a top-tier "Immortality Club." The series explores themes of mortality, power, and the price of eternal life through a modern urban fantasy lens with secret organization building at its core.

## Story & World

The story unfolds in a modern urban setting where Lu Li discovers the ability to manipulate lifespans and time itself. What starts as a personal power fantasy quickly evolves into complex empire-building as he establishes the "Eternal Club," recruiting members and building an organization that operates in the shadows of society. The world-building blends contemporary reality with supernatural elements, where life and death become commodities that can be traded and manipulated.

## Characters

**Lu Li (Protagonist)** - An ordinary worker turned mastermind after gaining the ability to manipulate life and lifespan. His transformation from pushover to strategic leader happens quickly‚Äîperhaps too quickly‚Äîbut his cautious, long-term approach to building the Immortality Club makes him an engaging protagonist despite the rushed character development.

## Art & Presentation

Full-color manhua with a long-strip vertical scrolling format typical of Chinese webcomics. The art is solid and clean, effectively conveying both the modern urban setting and the supernatural elements. Panel composition is clear and easy to follow, with good use of color to set atmosphere and tone.

*Rating: 7.5/10*

## Themes & Impact

The series explores several compelling themes:
- **Value of Life**: What is life and time worth when they become tradeable commodities?
- **Power and Corruption**: How gaining god-tier abilities transforms an ordinary person
- **Mortality vs Immortality**: The price and consequences of eternal life
- **Empire Building**: Creating and maintaining a secret organization behind society's curtain
- **Spectacle over Substance**: Prioritizing "what if" scenarios and entertainment over deep character studies

## Personal Notes & Observations

### Progress Update - Chapter 37 (14th February 2026)

**Observations:**

- **The Hook:** The core concept is fantastic. The MC grinding through life until he gets the power to buy and sell "lifespans" and mess with time is a unique hook that immediately grabbed me.
- **MC's Transformation:** Lu Li goes from a pushover to a master strategist very quickly. It feels a bit rushed, but understandable‚Äîsuddenly having god-tier powers would definitely spike anyone's confidence.
- **Smart Usage of Abilities:** What's really engaging is that he's not just randomly using his powers; he's building an actual organization (the "Immortality Club"), thinking long-term, and being cautious. The business-building aspect is satisfying to watch.
- **Art & Atmosphere:** The full-color art is solid and gets the job done with clean panels. The supernatural elements add depth, and the tangible fear of death in this world makes the stakes feel real.

### Completion - Chapter 303 (16th February 2026)

**Final Observations:**

- **Killer Concept:** The Eternal Club hits you with an absolutely chef's kiss premise right from the start‚Äîbuying and selling lifespans, time manipulation, building a secret club for immortals. It's the kind of high-concept idea that immediately hooks you and makes you go "okay, I'm IN."

## Verdict

**The Eternal Club** is ambitious popcorn entertainment that nails the concept but fumbles the execution. The premise‚Äîbuying and selling lifespans, time manipulation, building a secret immortality club‚Äîis absolutely killer and hooks you immediately. It's the kind of high-concept story that makes you lean in and say "tell me more."

The problem is, the MC's character development feels like it's on fast-forward. One moment he's getting kicked around, and the next he's a master strategist pulling off elaborate schemes without really earning that growth. It's jarring, but if you're willing to suspend disbelief and just enjoy the ride, there's a lot to love here.

The secret organization angle is addictive‚Äîwatching the MC build his empire piece by piece, manipulating time and trading lives behind society's curtain, scratches that power fantasy itch perfectly. The art is solid, the pacing keeps you engaged, and the world-building around immortality and lifespan trading is genuinely unique for the genre.

Don't come here expecting deep character studies or ironclad logic. This is entertainment that prioritizes spectacle and "what if" scenarios over realism. But if you're in the mood for a time-manipulation empire-building story with a fresh twist on immortality, you'll probably binge through this. It's fun, it's ambitious, and it attempts things most manhuas wouldn't dare.

**Bottom line:** A flawed but entertaining ride with a concept that deserves more credit. Worth reading if you can vibe with the power fantasy and forgive the rushed character development.

*Final Rating:* **7.10/10** ‚Äì Popcorn entertainment with ambition
