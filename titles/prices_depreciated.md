## Overview

*Prices Depreciated, and I Became the World’s Richest Person* follows the story of a protagonist who awakens in a world where the value of money has drastically changed—all prices have depreciated by a million times. However, his personal funds remain completely unchanged. Overnight, he instantly becomes the wealthiest individual globally. The manhua explores how he navigates this new reality and utilizes his sudden, immense fortune.

## Story & World

*(To be updated as I continue reading)*

The premise is straightforward but offers a lot of comedic and slice-of-life potential. Waking up to find that everyday items now cost fractions of a cent while keeping your previous bank balance intact is the ultimate wish-fulfillment scenario. However, the execution falls flat. Instead of focusing on the fun aspects of being rich, the story introduces far too many unnecessary problems and conflicts for the protagonist to solve, bogging down the pacing. There are numerous plot holes and broken events that break the immersion, and absurd inconsistencies—like the protagonist remaining the richest person in the world but still wearing the same basic attire without upgrading his lifestyle in logical ways.

## Characters

**Su Jingyang** - The protagonist who suddenly finds himself as the richest man in the world due to the global price depreciation. His reactions to the new economic reality and how he chooses to spend his wealth drive the narrative, though his character decisions often lack logic or consistency befitting his newfound status.

## Verdict

*Prices Depreciated, and I Became the World’s Richest Person* is a textbook example of a fantastic premise ruined by poor execution. The concept of global price depreciation while retaining personal wealth is a brilliant hook for a comedy/slice-of-life manhua, but the story continually shoots itself in the foot.

Rather than leaning into the fun, absurd wish-fulfillment aspects of the premise, the narrative bombards the protagonist with a never-ending string of problems to solve. This constant conflict fatigue completely derails the pacing and tone. Furthermore, the world-building and character logic are incredibly weak—the protagonist is globally wealthy yet inexplicably maintains his basic attire and lifestyle, completely breaking immersion. Combined with numerous plot holes and broken narrative events, the series quickly turns from an exciting new read into a frustrating slog. 

It gets a 5.2/10 almost entirely on the strength of its initial concept, but the story progression leaves much to be desired.
