## Overview

*Tales of Demons and Gods* follows Nie Li, the strongest Demon Spiritist in the world, who dies in battle against the Sage Emperor. He is then reborn as his 13-year-old self, back in the small city of Glory City, with all the knowledge and experience of his past life intact. Armed with future knowledge, he sets out to protect his city, save his loved ones, and change the fate of the world — all while climbing from the bottom of the cultivation ladder to the top.

## Story & World

The premise is a classic cultivation regression: a supremely powerful expert is reborn with all his knowledge and races to undo the tragedies of his past life. The early arcs are genuinely compelling — watching Nie Li use his future knowledge to rapidly ascend through cultivation ranks, uncover hidden resources, and outmaneuver enemies who don't know what's coming is satisfying in all the right ways.

The world of Glory City and its surrounding demon beast territories is well-constructed in the opening arcs. The cultivation system, the inscription patterns, the spirit beast integration — it all feels fleshed out and interesting. However, as the story progresses beyond Glory City into larger realms, the worldbuilding becomes increasingly shallow. New power levels, new realms, and new enemies are introduced at a pace that outstrips the story's ability to develop any of them meaningfully.

The elephant in the room: the original web novel by Mad Snail has been on indefinite hiatus since the mid-2010s. The manhua has continued beyond the novel's stopping point, but the lack of a completed source material is palpable. Later arcs feel aimless, and the narrative has no clear endgame.

## Characters

**Nie Li (Protagonist)** — Clever, strategic, and confident in the early arcs when his future knowledge creates genuine tension and payoff. Unfortunately, as his power scales up and the challenges stop feeling meaningful, he becomes more of a generic OP protagonist. The charm of watching him outthink his enemies diminishes when everything becomes "I'm just stronger now."

**Supporting Cast** — The early cast around Glory City is well-developed: Du Ze, Lu Piao, Ye Ziyun, and the other classmates have real personality. Once the story moves beyond Glory City, the side characters become increasingly interchangeable.

## Art & Presentation

*Rating: 6.5/10*

Full-color manhua in vertical scrolling format. The art was serviceable for its era but hasn't aged as well as competitors. Character designs are distinct enough in the early arcs but become harder to differentiate as the cast expands. Action sequences are functional but rarely spectacular.

## Themes & Impact

- **Regression and Second Chances**: Using future knowledge to prevent tragedy
- **Protecting Home**: The emotional weight of defending Glory City and its people
- **Knowledge as Power**: Cultivation through understanding, not just brute force
- **The Cost of Incomplete Stories**: What happens when a narrative loses its direction

## Personal Notes & Observations

### Progress Update — On Hold (11th July 2024, Ch. 473)

Read 473 chapters over about two months. The first ~200 chapters are genuinely excellent — the Glory City arc is peak cultivation regression done right. Nie Li's strategic use of future knowledge, the inscription patterns, the demon beast integration system — it all clicks. But the further you get from Glory City, the more the series loses its identity. New realms and power levels get introduced without the development that made the early arcs compelling.

The fact that the novel is on indefinite hiatus (courtesy of Mad Snail's infamous track record) casts a shadow over everything. The manhua continues, but it's clearly operating without a roadmap, and it shows. Put on hold partly because of this uncertainty and partly because the quality declined enough that the urgency to keep reading disappeared.

## Verdict

*(Interim verdict at Ch. 473 — to be revised if the story ever concludes)*

*Tales of Demons and Gods* is a textbook case of "the beginning was amazing." The Glory City arc — roughly the first 200 chapters — is among the best cultivation regression stories in manhua. Nie Li is sharp, the worldbuilding is detailed, and the power system is creative. Watching him use inscription patterns and demon beast knowledge to rapidly climb the ranks is exactly the kind of satisfying progression that makes this genre addictive.

Then the story leaves Glory City, and everything that made it special starts to dilute. New realms, new power levels, new enemies — but none with the depth or development that made the first arc so compelling. And hanging over everything is the knowledge that Mad Snail — an author notorious for abandoning stories — has left the novel on indefinite hiatus since the mid-2010s. The manhua soldiers on, but you can feel the lack of a completed roadmap in every aimless arc.

At 473 chapters, there's enough good material to justify the read, especially the early arcs. But the diminishing returns and the uncertainty of ever getting a proper conclusion make it hard to recommend without heavy caveats.

**Bottom line:** A genuinely excellent cultivation regression beginning that gradually loses its way, made worse by the novel's indefinite hiatus.

## Additional Context

Based on the web novel by Mad Snail (发飙的蜗牛), which has been on indefinite hiatus since the mid-2010s with approximately 484 chapters published. The manhua adaptation by Jiang Ruotai has continued beyond the novel's stopping point and is still ongoing. First published in 2015, making it one of the older cultivation manhuas still technically in serialization.
